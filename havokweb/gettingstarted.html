Getting Started on Havok
Created By Greg Hovey for Havok Mud

Definition
First off, for those of you that don’t know what a MUD is, a MUD is a Text Based Multi-User-Dungeon or Dimension where many people from all around the world can play. No software or big expensive programs are needed to play. All you need is a basic Telnet program which most systems come equipped with. But if you truly want the full mudding experience, a MUD client like Zmud, which is available at www.zuggsoft.com, is advisable but that can all be done later after trying it with the standard Telnet program and after deciding if MUD’ing is for you.


Connecting
Ok, to connect, the simplest way if your using Windows is to Click on the Start Menu and click run and enter the line ‘Telnet havok.haaksman.org 6969’ or enter this line ‘telnet://havok.haaksman.org:6969/’ into your Internet browser.
Upon doing this, a black or white window should appear along with a big Dragon with the words Havok across it. Below that, it should prompt you for a User name. Be Creative now, something Medieval and Unique. No using any D&D names or names such as Bilbo or any other well known name. After confirming your name and your password, you’ll be sent to the Character Creation Menu.

Character Creation
In order to complete this section, you must select a Gender, Race, Class, and your Stats for you character from this menu. If you need any help or hints regarding any of these menus, then please select the Race and Class help option to the left on this web browser. (not on your telnet client) There may be some help, built into the game.

Entering the World
Upon selecting the Done Option in that menu, you’ll be booted to the MOTD(Message of the day as well as a few getting started tips. After reading up on those, you’ll be moved to another menu where you can Enter or Quit the game. Choose to enter the game and you’ll be warped to the Temple of Karysinya where your fun will begin.

You can obtain this information about the room your in by typing ‘LOOK’. Upon inspecting the Exits in the room, you can move to other rooms by typing the direction name (North, South, East, West, Up, and Down). From here, you can venture West where you can try your luck at slaying some evil creatures, go East into donations where some free equipment and items are, go north into the hall of rules or finally, venture southward into Havok’s wonderful world.

Communications
Now adventuring and slaying evil beast (and some good ones), may be a very essential part of the game but another aspect not to be over looked is the Communications and interaction with the other people in the game. To see who else is playing Havok, type ‘who’. Now in order to talk to talk or communication with people, they should be relatively close to you. Type ‘whozone’ to see who is nearest to you. There is various means of communications throughout Havok.
Say <Text> - This form of communications is room only. Only people in the same room as you will hear you talking
Talk <Person> <Text> - This is a means of private communications between 2 people. You can pretty much only talk to people in the same zone as you or to any immortal in the game.
Yell <Text> - Yell is used to communication to everyone in the same zone as you. This is probably the best bet to get help if you need it starting off. Just gossip Can I get some help or something to that nature.
Shout <Text> - This is probably the most popular channel, upon shouting, anyone in the world can hear you but you must be level 2 in order to shout.


These are pretty much the 4 essential means of communications. Just a note though, please, no swearing over public channels. We here at HavokMUD try to provide a fun, PG13 type atmosphere for all to enjoy.

Game Play
Now, if you decided to venture westward from the temple, you’ll be brought to a very simple zone/area for you to explore. You should find some basic enemies that you can battle with. To see how strong they are type ‘Consider <Mob Name>’ which should give you a good indication to how strong they are. Now to start your battles, type ‘Kill <Mob Name>’. After slaying the beast or monster, you should obtain some experience that goes towards gaining another level. To see how much more experience you need to level or any other basic stat, type ‘Score’. 

Also after finishing off your foe, his corpse and equipment he was using is up for grabs. To see what he has, type ‘Examine corpse or look in corpse’. If anything catches your eye in the corpse, type ‘Get <item> corpse or get all corpse’.

After finishing off this zone, you should have enough experience to gain a level, which will make you stronger. You must find your guild master though in order to obtain your level. The guild masters are normally in the guild master’s row, which is located south, east, east, 4 north and westwardly. Before going west, please note that this is the Meeting Place where many people congest together and talk. Also note that the innkeeper is located east and up from here where everyone must go to RENT once they would like to leave. They will keep your equipment and character nice and safe from thieves and such.

Now once you find the correct guildmaster, type ‘Gain’ to obtain your new level. You can also practice new skills and spells from this guy. Type ‘Practice’ to see this list and ‘Practice <skill or spell>’ to learn it.

Conclusion
Anyway, this should give you enough knowledge to get you rolling and playing. Play around with the ‘Help’ command to learn more of Havok’s commands and options. There should also be lots of people around to help you so just ask around and stick with it. MUDs are a very fun and addictive game so have fun and Enjoy!
