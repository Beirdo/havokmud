Newbie Guide: Levels, Skills, and Spells
 

Once you've earned enough experience for your first level, you have to pay a visit to your friendly Guildmaster.  To find your GM, follow these directions:

From the Temple of Karsinya, go south, two rooms east, four rooms north.  (In shorthand, that's s2e4n.)  This will leave you at the Meeting Square, which is the central room in Karsinya.  The Karsinya Guild Hall begins one room west from the Meeting Square - most of the low level GMs are there.  Explore the Hall and find yours.

Exceptions:
The Druid GM is uncomfortable in the city, and so he spends his days in the forest.  It'll take some looking to find him, but if you leave town from the West Gate, he's in the forest, in a southwesterly direction.

The Monk GM prefers to spend his time in the Monestary, which (from the Meeting Square) is s5w4n5w4n.  He'll be down the hallway to the east.

When you're standing in the same room as your GM, type gain and you'll gain your level.  Type practice to see a list of the skills and spells you can learn at your level.  Type practice (or prac) followed by a skill to learn that skill, like this: prac kick or prac cure light.

Using skills and casting spells
Nearly every non-magical skill is used simply by typing in the name, for instance, to use the find food skill (which is a Ranger ability), you type find food.  Spells, however, work a little differently.  If your character is a Mage or a Cleric, you have to use the cast command, like this: cast 'bless' self, or cast 'cure critic' bilbo.  Note that Mage and Cleric spells must have a quote mark on either side of it.

If your character is a Psionist, however, s/he has to use the mind command to use her abilities: mind 'telekenesis' frodo.
