Tips and Tricks

  For a complete walk-through, check out the Guide to Building on Havok: these pages are intended as quick-references.

 

Basics first:

           Sector types and room flags To easily check your sector types and room flags, use show r (zone#).  It’ll list every room that you have, including vnum, room name, sector type, and room flags.  It’s good to go through this occasionally, but especially when you think you’re just about done with your zone, to make sure everything there makes sense.

           Use stat room and get used to how it looks so you can look things up with it quickly.  It’s a very valuable command, both for getting reference information, and also for doublechecking your work.

           Wandering mobs If you’re making a mob that’s not flagged sentinel, it WILL wander around your zone while you’re working on it.  One way to keep it still is to work on it in your homeroom (wizset home), and making sure that your homeroom has no exits.  Another is to mforce sleep your mob so it goes to sleep while you’re operating.

           SAVE FREQUENTLY Use rsave to save your rooms.  I rsave pretty much every time I modify a room, and I do a mass rsave (rsave (startvnum) (endvnum)) periodically throughout a building session.   The word ‘rsave’ should be ingrained into your fingers, so that they’ll type it on their own every time a room gets changed.  The buildsite doesn’t crash as much as it used to, but it still happens, and you’ll lose all the work you’ve done if you don’t save.

           Spellchecking If your spelling or grammar is lacking (or if you’re a non-native English speaker), you should be considerate and use Word or some other program with a spellchecker to write your descs.  Once you have them, you can copy them into the game.  When a zone gets finished, I go through it room by room, checking for spelling errors, funky grammar, as well as mob and item balance issues.  If I find lots of mistakes, I will more than likely just stop checking and tell you to go through and fix them all yourself.  So please do.
