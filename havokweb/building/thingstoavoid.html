Things to Absolutely Positively Avoid

  For a complete walk-through, check out the Guide to Building on Havok: these pages are intended as quick-references.

 

XI.1: Weather/Time of Day

            You should not ever refer to weather conditions or anything pertaining to the time of day in your room descs.  The reason is that, unless your room is flagged and sector type’d INSIDE, the player will be getting generic weather spam that will probably contradict what you’ve written.  The only possible exception is if you get one of our wonderous coders to write you a specific proc for your zone that will give certain weather/time of day messages.

 

XI.2: Objectivity

            This is a big one.  The most important.  Your room descs should not ever tell the player what s/he’s thinking, feeling, or anything else that’s personal, including reactions to things.  For instance, a player comes across a graveyard that’s been desecrated and has corpses rotting out in the open.  A paladin is going to be extremely distrought and find the smell abhorrant (as will most classes), but a necromancer might actually like it, and take some enjoyment out of the sight.  Hell, he’ll probably roll up his sleeves and start making some zombies.  This is to say, your room descs should be OBJECTIVE, and accurately portrait what’s going on in the room, regardless of who might be reading it.

 

XI.3: YOU YOU YOU YOU YOU YOU YOU

            This is the most common mistake made by new builders, and it can absolutely destroy an otherwise good zone.  Your room descs should not contain the word “you” in them.  There are very seldom when the context of the word makes it passably okay, but really you should strive to not use it.  If you do, in fact, use the word ‘you’ in your descs, you’ll have to go through when you’re all done and change your descs to take them out.  This can be a considerable amount of work, so the best thing to do is not do it in the first place.

            Advice:

            Usually, you can avoid using ‘you’ just by rearranging the sentence.  For instance, here’s a bad example:

                        As you walk into the temple, the first thing you notice is the giant crucifix hanging from the wall.  You can feel eyes staring at you from the shadows, and it makes you feel very uncomfortable.

            Here’s one possible way to rewrite this objectively:

                        A giant crucifix dominates the northern wall of the temple, hanging from the wooden rafters by steel wires.  A feeling of watchfullness fills the room, as if some being is keeping constant watch, maintaining vigil against intruders.

            The main point to take from this is that you should never tell your players what they’re doing.  They’re the one’s playing the game, let them decide.

 

XI.4: Implied Direction of Travel

            A bad habit that’s very easy to slip into is to start writing your roomdescs as if the player is coming from one direction and leaving from the other.  While it is true that on some occasions, particularly the first time through, they will be coming in and going out by those directions, it will not always be true.  This is related to the whole objectivity thing.

            Here’s an example of what I’m talking about:

                        Having crossed the bridge, the path leads you north to what seems to be a large stone church.  What surprises could the church hold in store for you?  Who can tell…

            Okay, well if you’ve been keeping up, you’ll notice two or three other things wrong with this example than just the implied direction of travel.  That’s a good point: once you master describing a room as opposed to telling someone about a room, you’ll find it easier and easier to avoid this mistake.

 

XI.5: Deathtraps

Deathtraps can help to make a zone interesting, but don’t use tons of em.  Many zones have none at all, and only the hardest zones should have more than one.  If you DO use one, some sense of danger should be imparted to the players in the rooms that lead into the DT.  Under no circumstances should a DT exist which is given without warning and without reason.  This just makes the game not fun.
