Mob and Object Placement

  For a complete walk-through, check out the Guide to Building on Havok: these pages are intended as quick-references.

 

            This subject doesn’t really need its own chapter, because it’s one of the simplest parts of building.  Basically, when you’ve got your mobs and objects done and ready to put in the zone, just go around and put them where you want them.  Use load m and load o to load up your mobs and objects.  If you have any mobs are are not flagged sentinel, keep in mind that they will be wandering around between when you load them and when you save.  To save mob and object locations in a zone, use zsave (zone #).  This will save the location of every mob and object currently in your zone. 

Keep in mind that zsave does not discriminate between mobs and objects that you want in the zone, and mobs and objects that somehow magically appeared there.  Trust me, it happens.  Especially on your first zone or two.  As I said in the chapters on item and mob creation, ALWAYS DESTROY THE FIRST COPY OF ANY NEW MOB OR OBJECT.  If you zsave an item or a mob into your zone, and the one you use is the first one that you made, the mud will remember what that item or mob used to be, and that old item/mob will load there instead of the new one. 

If you want a mob to have an item in his inventory, give it to him.  If you want him to be wearing it, mforce him to wear it, although sometimes, especially with weapons, the mob’s natural stats are better than what he gets with the item equipped, and he’ll automatically take it off.  If you really want him to be wearing it, tinker with his stats a bit.

If you’ve got items in containers, like a treasure chest, put the items in the chest, lock it (if necessary), and put the chest on the ground, then zsave.

Keep in mind that if you ‘quit now’ to get out of the game, any items you have in your inventory will fall onto the ground, and they can be zsaved if you don’t notice them later.

Also keep in mind that zsave is used to save certain conditions of doors: namely, whether or not the door is closed, and whether or not it is locked.
